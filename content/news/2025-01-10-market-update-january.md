# January 2025 Market Update: Strong Start to the Year

The Greater Springfield area real estate market is demonstrating robust activity to begin 2025, with encouraging trends across all three counties served by GSBOR.

## Key Market Highlights

### Sales Activity

- **Total Units Sold**: 342 homes (15% increase from January 2024)
- **Average Days on Market**: 28 days (improvement from 35 days last year)
- **Inventory Levels**: 2.1 months of supply (balanced market conditions)

### Pricing Trends

- **Median Sale Price**: $185,000 (3.2% increase year-over-year)
- **Average Sale Price**: $205,500 (2.8% increase year-over-year)
- **Price Per Square Foot**: $112 (stable from previous year)

## County-Specific Performance

### Greene County

Greene County continues to lead the market with 198 closed sales, representing a 12% increase from January 2024. The median sale price of $195,000 reflects the county's strong demand and limited inventory.

### Christian County

Christian County showed impressive growth with 89 closed sales, marking an 18% increase year-over-year. The area's affordability with a median price of $168,000 continues to attract first-time homebuyers.

### Webster County

Webster County maintained steady performance with 55 closed sales and a median price of $175,000, showing the area's consistent appeal to buyers seeking value.

## Market Outlook

Several factors are contributing to the positive market conditions:

1. **Low Interest Rates**: Continued favorable lending conditions
2. **Job Growth**: Springfield area employment expansion
3. **Population Growth**: Steady in-migration to the region
4. **Limited Inventory**: Balanced supply and demand
